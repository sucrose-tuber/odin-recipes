DESCRIPTION

My uncultured friends always describe my breakfast as "weird" when I serve this to them. In reality, it's a very common dish in Latin America. I will assume in Africa as well because the only photo I could find available for public use was written in Igbo.

INGREDIENTS

  • Banana or plaintain
  • 1 or 2 eggs, depending how hungry you are
  • Half a can of beans because I never remember to soak uncooked beans overnight
  • Oil, preferably vegetable (lighter flavor compared to others)
  • Shredded cheese
  • Tabasco sauce (red if you like spicy, green if you like flavor, or both)
  1. Boil the canned beans if you like them extra soft, otherwise heating them in a microwaveable bowl works if you don't have time
  2. Cut banana/plaintains into slices
  3. Fry the slices with vegetable oil
  4. Set them aside on your plate
  5. Fry your egg the way you like (I personally like scrambled for this specific recipe)
  6. In your bowl, layer the cooked ingredients in this order: beans, Tabasco sauce, shredded cheese, egg, plaintain
  7. I try to eat it like a cake so I can get all the flavors but you can also mix the ingredients
